# StreetSafe India

### *A Youth-Led Public Policy & Public Health Initiative*

StreetSafe India is a youth-led initiative focused on addressing **street dog safety, rabies prevention, and urban animal welfare** through legal research, systems thinking, and community engagement.

We aim to develop **balanced, evidence-based policy frameworks** that protect both:
- Human public safety  
- Animal welfare and humane treatment  

---

## The Core Problem

India faces a complex urban challenge:

- Millions of dog bite cases annually (WHO)
- Risk of rabies transmission
- Inconsistent implementation of sterilization and vaccination programs
- Legal tension between public safety and animal protection laws

This creates a governance gap between **law, policy, and implementation**.

---

## 📜 Legal Framework

StreetSafe India examines this issue through three intersecting systems:

### Public Safety (Article 21 – Right to Life)
Governments have a constitutional duty to protect citizens from preventable harm and ensure public health infrastructure.

### Animal Welfare Laws
Cruelty against animals is prohibited, and humane treatment is required under national legislation.

### Municipal Responsibility
Local authorities are responsible for:
- vaccination programs  
- sterilization (ABC Rules 2001)  
- waste management  
- population monitoring  

---

## Our Policy Framework

StreetSafe India proposes a dual-rights system:

### 1. Vaccination & Sterilization Expansion
- 70%+ coverage target (WHO benchmark)
- Annual rabies vaccination drives
- Tracking and monitoring systems

### 2. Community Dog Zones
- Designated feeding and monitoring areas
- Reduced human-animal conflict zones

### 3. Legal Clarity Protocols
- Clear guidelines for aggressive animals
- Standardized relocation procedures
- Emergency response systems

### 4. Accountability Systems
- Municipal performance tracking
- Public reporting mechanisms
- Enforcement of compliance standards
